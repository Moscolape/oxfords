TOPIC : BALANCE SHEET

SALIENT POINTS

The balance sheet is a statement of the financial position of a business. It is therefore, a report showing the status of the business.

It is a statement of an individual or business that shows the assets, liabilities and capital.

CONTENTS

Items contained in a balance sheet are assets, liabilities and capital.

It is a statement of an individual or business that shows the assets, liabilities and capital.

USES OF BALANCE SHEET

1.) It is used to show:

(a.) what the business owns. (Assets)

(b.) what the business owes others. (Liabilities)

(c.) what the business worth on a specific date.

(Assets - Liabilities =Capital).

2.) It is a statement showing the financial position of a business or the financial status of the business.

3.) The balance sheet is used in summarizing the balance remaining in the ledger after the preparation of the profit and loss account.

CLASSIFICATION OF BALANCE SHEET ITEMS

In classification of items we have:

- Assets

- Fixed Assets

- Current Assets or floating/Circulating Assets

- Liabilities

- Long-term liabilities

- Current Liabilities

- Payable accounts

- Capital and Liabilities

- Capital

DIFFERENCES BETWEEN VERTICAL AND HORIZONTAL BALANCE SHEETS

Horizontal Balance Sheet Vertical Balance Sheet
a.) Assets are shown on the right hand side and the liabilities on the left hand side. Assets are shown at the top and then capital and liabilities are shown underneath in instead of side by side.
b.) One needs knowledge of accounting to understand the balance sheet. It is easier to understand the balance sheet.
