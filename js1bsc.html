CLASS : JS1

TOPIC : FORCES

BREAKDOWN : MEANING AND TYPES, EXAMPLES OF ITS TYPES

LEARNING OUTCOME : Explain force, name the types of forces, and name the examples of contact and non-contact forces.


FORCE

A force is defined as that which changes the shape, speed or direction of a moving body. There are two types of force;

a.) Contact force

b.) Non-contact force

CONTACT FORCE

A contact force is a force applied directly to an object. It may involve pushing or pulling.

Examples:

i.) stretching the sling of a catapult.

ii.) twisting a washed cloth to remove water from it.

iii.) tensional and frictional forces.

NON-CONTACT FORCE

Non-contact forces are the forces whose sources do not require contact with the body to which they are applied. Such forces are called force fields. Examples include:

i.) Magnetic force

ii.) Electric force

iii.) Gravitational force

BALANCED AND UNBALANCED FORCES

A force is said to be balanced when all the forces acting on a body are equal and opposite. If the forces acting on it are not equal, the forces are unbalanced and the body will move to the direction of this unbalanced force.

ASSIGNMENT

1.) Define 'force'.

2.) Name the two(2) types of force.

3.) Explain the two(2) types of force with atleast two(2) examples each.

4.) Briefly explain when a force is balanced and unbalanced.
