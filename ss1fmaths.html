CENTRAL TENDENCY

A statistical measure which describes the middle or centre of a set of data is called a measure of central tendency. There are many measures of central tendency but the commonest are; the arithmetic mean, the median and the mode.

THE ARITHMETIC MEAN

The arithmetic mean is the most important measure of central tendency because it makes use of all members of a given set. If the members of the set are represented by x1, x2, x3,..., xn, the mean usually denoted by:

THE MEDIAN

The median of a set of numbers, n, is defined as the 1/2(n+1)th value when the numbers are arranged in the order of magnitude, that is, according to their size.

THE MODE

The mode of a distribution is the value which occurs most frequently in the distribution. In a grouped frequency distribution, the class with the highest frequency is called the modal class

QUESTION 2

Given a set of numbers: 104, 107, 110, 103, 118, 110, find:

(a.) The median (b.) The mode

SOLUTION

(a.) Arranging these numbers in order of magnitude, we have: 103, 104, 107,110, 110, 118.

There are 6 numbers in the set. The median is the 1/2(6+1)th number which is the 7/2th number = 3.5th number.

The 3.5th number is the average of the 3rd and 4th numbers.

therefore, the median = 107+110 / 2 = 217/2 = 108.5
