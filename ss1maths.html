RATIO

Ratio is a comparison of two or more quantities of the same magnitude or kind. The units of these quantities are meant to be the same and where they are not, appropriate conversion can be carried out.

EXAMPLE 1

Express the ratio of N1.20 to 72kobos as simply as possible.

SOLUTION

To solve this problem, we have to express N1.20 in kobo.

Thus N1.20 = 100k x 1.20 = 120k

So ratio of N1.20 to 72kobo = 120k : 72k = 1205/ 723 = 5/3

therefore ratio = 5:3

COMPARISON OF RATIOS

When a ratio is expressed in the form of k:1, in which k is a whole number, a decimal fraction or a vulgar fraction, then it is used for comparison.

EXAMPLE 2

Express 57:19 in the form k:1.

SOLUTION

The ratio 57:19 can be written as:

57/19 = 573/191 = 3/1

therefore, the ratio = 3:1

RATE

Rate is the comparison of two or more quantities of different kinds. Suppose a worker is paid N210 for a 7-hours-per-day work, his rate of pay is (N210/7) per hour which is N30 per hour.

EXAMPLE 3

At the rate of 2 cupfuls per day, a sack of yam-flour lasts 69 days. How long will 3 sacks of yam-flour last when the consumption is 9 cupfuls per day?

SOLUTION

To solve this problem, let us assume that it is the same number of people that will eat the yam-flour. It implies that more sacks will last more days.

So the ratio of the sacks = 3:1 = 3/1

Also, more cups per day implies that the sack would last fewer days.

So the ratio of the cupfuls = 2:9 = 2/9

therefore 3 sacks at the rate of 9 cupfuls per day will last for:

=3/1 x 2/9 x 69 days
= 31/1 x 2/ 93 x 69 days
= 1/1 x 2/ 31 x 6923 days
= 1 x 2 x 23days
= 46 days

PROPORTION

Proportion is simply defined as the equality of ratios.

INTERIOR AND EXTERIOR ANGLES OF TRIANGLES AND POLYGONS

In this lesson, we are going to state and prove one of the theorems on triangles. For any theorem to be proved in Mathematics, certain procedures are to be followed:

i.) Represent the theorem diagrammatically.

ii.) State what is given mathematically.

iii.) State what is to be proved.

iv.) Indicate the constructions made if any.

v.) State the proof.

Now, let us look at this theorem on triangles.

Theorem: The sum of the angles of a triangle is 1800

Given: Any triangle ABC
To prove: < ABC + < BAC + < ACB = 1800
Construction: Extend |BC| to point D. Draw |CE| parallel to |BA|.
Proof: From the above figure;

a = e (alt. angles , BA||CE )

b = d (corr angles , BA||CE )

c + e + d = 1800 (sum of angles on a straight line)

therefore, c + a + b = 1800

=> a + b + c = 1800

and < ABC + < BAC + < ACB = 1800

QUESTION 1

Find the interior angles of a regular polygon of 18 sides.

SOLUTION

To find the interior < of a regular polygon, we can find the exterior angles first and then subtract your result from 1800 or you can use the formular for sum of int. angles of a polygon i.e (2n - 4) x 900.

Let us use the first approach.

Exterior angles = 3600/n
n = no. of sides of the polygon = 18

therefore exterior angles = 3600 / 18 = 200

Int. angles + Ext. angles = 1800

Int. angles + 200 = 1800

Int. angles = 1800 - 200

therefore, the interior angles of the polygon = 1600

CONGRUENT TRIANGLES

Conditions for congruency of two triangles:

i.) Two sides and the included angle are equal...SAS

ii.) Three corresponding equal sides...SSS

iii.) Two angles and the included side...ASA

iv.) The right angle, hypotenuse and another side...RHS

ISOSCELES AND EQUILATERAL TRIANGLES

An Isosceles triangle is a triangle in which 2 sides are equal and also, the base angles are equal.

An Equilateral triangle is a triangle with all three sides and angles equal.

QUESTION 2

An isosceles triangle is such that the vertical angle is 4 times the size of each of the base angles. What is the size of each of the base angles?

SOLUTION

Let each of the base angles of the isosceles triangle be x0

Then the vertical angle = 4x0

Then, x + x + 4x = 1800 (sum of angles in a triangle)

6x = 1800

Divide bothsides by the coefficient of x which is 6

6x/6 = 1800/6

6x/6 = 180030/6

x = 300

=> the size of each of the base angles = 300
