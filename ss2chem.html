TOPIC : SATURATED HYDROCARBON

BREAKDOWN : HOMOLOGOUS SERIES, ALKYL AND FUNCTIONAL GROUPS

LEARNING OUTCOME : Define homologous series, state characteristics of homologous series, explain alkyl and functional groups.


HOMOLOGOUS SERIES

A homologous series is a family of organic compounds which follows a regular structural pattern in which each successive member differs in its molecular formula by a -CH2- group and in its relative molecular mass by 14.

CHARACTERISTICS OF HOMOLOGOUS SERIES

All members of a particular homologous series have the following characteristics:

1.) General molecular formula.

2.) Difference in molecular formula -CH2-.

3.) Difference in relative molecular mass, 14.

4.) Similar chemical properties.

5.) Physical properties depend on number of carbon atoms.

6.) General method of preparation.

Examples of homologous series are: alkanes, alkenes, alkynes, alkanols, alkones, alkanals, alkanoates, alkanoic acids e.t.c

ALKYL GROUP

Alkyl groups consist of all univalent group derived from the alkanes from by the loss of a hydrogen atom. Examples are methyl(CH3), ethyl(C2H5), propyl(C3H7), butyl(C4H9) e.t.c

General formula for alkyl groups - (CnH2n+1)

FUNCTIONAL GROUP

A functional group is an atom, a radical or a bond common to a homologous series and which determines the main chemical properties of a series.

Examples of functional groups are hydroxyl group(OH), carboxylic group(COOH), single bond(-), double bond(=), triple bond e.t.c

Note: Saturated compounds are compounds containing atoms joined only by single covalent bonds e.g alkanes
