LEARNING OUTCOME : Explain relative frequency, explain sample space and event space, explain the probability of an event, state what equiprobable sample space is, find the probability of an event.


RELATIVE FREQUENCY

Suppose we toss a coin a certain number of times and we denote the number of times we are successful in getting a head by n(s) and we denote the total number of trials by n(t). Then the ratio n(s)/n(t) is called the Relative Frequency of the number of times a head shows up.

SAMPLE SPACE AND EVENT SPACE

When a random experiment is carried out, the result of such an experiment is usually called an outcome. Thus, the set of all possible outcomes of any random experiment is referred to as the sample space while a subset of the sample space which may be a collection of outcomes of a random experiment is called an event space.

Let us use S to denote sample space and n(S) to denote the number of outcomes in S. May we equally use E to represent event space and n(E) to represent number of outcomes in E.

Thus, the probability of an event, E, denoted by P(E) is defined as:
P(E) = n(E)/n(S).

Generally, the probability of any event lies between 0 and 1 inclusive. i.e 0 <= P(E) <= 1

EQUIPROBABLE SAMPLE SPACE

In the experiment of throwing an unbiased coin, a head and a tail have equal chance of appearing. Here the probabilty of a head appearing is equal to the probability of a tail appearing which is 1/2. The same is applicable to the tossing of a fair die which has 6 faces. Each of them has an equal chance of appearing. Here, the probability of each face appearing is 1/6. Thus, the sample space to which we assign each of the sample elements' equal probability is called an equiprobable sample space.

EXAMPLE 1

In a single throw of two unbiased coins, find the probability that:

a.) two heads appear

b.) one head and one tail appear

SOLUTION

Let S be the sample space
Then S = {HH, TT, HT, TH}
So n(S) = 4

a.) Let E1 be the event the two heads appear

Then E1 = {HH}
So n(E1) = 1

Therefore, P(E1) = n(E1)/n(S) = 1/4 = 0.25

b.) Let E2 be the event that one head and one tail appear.

Then E2 = {HT, TH}
So n(E2) = 2

Therefore, P(E2) = n(E2)/n(S) = 2/4 = 1/2 = 0.5
