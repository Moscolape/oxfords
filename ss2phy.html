LEARNING OUTCOME : Define surface tension, classify fluids, give atleast two(2) examples of the application of surface tension and viscosity.


SURFACE TENSION

Surface tension is defined as a force acting along surface of a liquid, causing the liquid surface to behave like a stretched elastic skin.

Many everyday activities show that the surface of a liquid behaves as if it were a stretched elastic. For example, when the drop of water forming slowly on a tap is observed, the surface of the water appears to behave like an elastic skin or bag supporting the weight of the water.

When a needle is placed on a small piece of filter paper on the surface of water, if the wet paper starts sinking, the needle will remain afloat.

An insect called a pond skater can walk across a water surface without sinking. All these show that the water surface is under tension, and acts like an elastic skin covering the surface.

EFFECTS OF SURFACE TENSION

1.) Some materials like umbrella, raincoats, and tents are treated with oil-based substances which prevents the wetting of such materials.

2.) It is difficult to wash effectively an oily plates or dirty cloth with only water.

HOW TO REDUCE SURFACE TENSION

The surface tension in water can be reduced in the folowing ways:

1.) Addition of detergent(soap).

2.) Increasing the temperature of the water(heating).

3.) Dissolving camphor in water.

4.) Addition of alcohol.

5.) Sprinkling of lycopodium powder(chemical) on the surface of the water.

HOW TO INCREASE SURFACE TENSION

The following increases surface tension in water:

1.) Impurities in water(dirts).

2.) Addition of Oil.


VISCOSITY

It is observed that it is easier to pour water or kerosene from a container than to pour honey or engine oil. A stone dropped into a bucket of water gets to the bottom faster than when the same stone is dropped into the same size of a bucket containing lubricating oil. These differences is due to the property of viscosity.

Definition: Viscosity is defined as the internal friction between layers of liquid or gas in motion.

Examples of Viscous Liquids

Liquids which pour slowly are known as viscous liquids. Such thick liquids like engine oil, thick palm oil, honey, crude oil e.t.c

Terminal Velocity

When a stone is dropped into a viscous liquid, it falls faster and faster until it reaches its maximum speed. At this point, the stone moves with a constant velocity because its acceleration is zero and the upward forces balance the downward force.

W-V-U = ma

W = weight(downward force), V = viscous force(upward force) and U = Upthrust(upward force)
